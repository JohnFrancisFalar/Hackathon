• The Red Planet: Mars is known as the "Red Planet" because of its reddish appearance caused by iron oxide (rust) on its surface.
  • Boiling Point of Water: Water boils at 100°C under normal atmospheric pressure.
  • Heart Function: The heart is the organ responsible for pumping blood throughout the body.
  • Oxygen for Survival: We inhale oxygen, which is essential for cellular respiration and life.
  • Water’s State: At room temperature, water is a liquid — not a solid or gas.
  • Earth's Satellite: The Moon is Earth's only natural satellite, orbiting our planet.
  • Sense of Sight: The eyes are the sense organs that allow us to see the world around us.
  • Center of the Solar System: The Sun is the center of our solar system, with planets orbiting it.
  • Bees and Nectar: Bees collect nectar from flowers, which they convert into honey.
  • Gravity: Gravity is the force that pulls objects toward Earth and keeps us grounded.
